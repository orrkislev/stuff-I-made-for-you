3D Text Through Lenses but What is it?

I created this sketch to show you how text looks when viewed through multiple refractive lenses. Using Three.js, I built an interactive 3D scene where you can "look around" text by moving your mouse, seeing it through a series of transparent spheres.

Key elements:
  • 3D text from a custom font
  • Adjustable spherical lenses
  • Mouse-controlled camera
 how does it work? You can tweak various parameters to change the visual effect:
  • Word: Change the displayed text
  • Lens count: Adjust the number of spheres
  • Lens size: Modify the size range of the spheres
  • Roughness: Alter the surface texture of the lenses
  • Transmission: Change how much light passes through
  • Thickness: Adjust the optical density
  • Colors: Modify text, lens, and background colors
These options let you explore different visual outcomes, from subtle magnification to extreme distortion. It's a blend of digital art and optics, showcasing how Three.js can create engaging 3D web graphics.
