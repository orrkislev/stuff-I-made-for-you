Study in Fluid Particles Introduction What is it?

Blobular Flow is a creative coding sketch that simulates the behavior of particle systems, visualizing them as organic, blob-like entities. The sketch features two families of particles, represented by pink and white blobs, that interact with each other and their environment in a fluid, dynamic manner.

Mouse Movement: Move your cursor around the sketch. Particles will be repelled from your mouse, creating dynamic interactions with the blobs.
Edges: Observe how particles bounce off the edges of the canvas, maintaining a border around the sketch.

 How it works?
Particle System: The core of the sketch is a particle system where each dot is an instance of the Dot class.
Spatial Indexing: A KD-Bush spatial index is used for efficient neighbor lookups.
Forces: Particles are subject to various forces:
  • - Attraction/repulsion between particles
  • - Repulsion from the mouse cursor
  • - A slight rotational force towards the center
Visualization: Particles are rendered as circles, then processed with blur and threshold filters to create the blob effect.
 Main Parameters
  • blobSize (default: 8): Controls the size of individual particles before blurring.
  • blurSize (default: 6): Determines the amount of blur applied to the particle rendering.
  • threshold (default: 0.4): Sets the cutoff point for the threshold filter, affecting blob shape and size.
  • border (default: 250): Defines the invisible boundary from the edge of the canvas.
  • backgroundColor (default: '#333'): Sets the background color of the sketch.
  • families (default: ['pink', 'white']): Defines the colors of the two particle families.
  • dotsPerFamily (default: 100): Sets the number of particles in each family.
Adjusting these parameters can dramatically change the appearance and behavior of the Blobular Flow, allowing for a wide range of visual effects and interactions.
